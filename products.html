Featured Products

Discover the water footprint of some of the most common products.

Rice

Water Footprint: 2,497 liters/kg "Rice, a staple food for more than half of the world's population, has a significant water footprint due to the paddy cultivation process. Flooded rice fields consume vast amounts of water, making it essential for us to appreciate every grain and minimize waste."

Jeans

Water Footprint: 7,600 liters/pair "The iconic blue jeans we all love come with a hidden cost. From the cotton fields to dyeing processes, producing a single pair of jeans can consume as much water as one person drinks in 7 years! Choosing quality over quantity and extending the life of our jeans can make a big difference."

Coffee

Water Footprint: 132 liters/cup"That invigorating cup of coffee that kickstarts our mornings has its roots in water-intensive coffee farms. Each cup embodies water used in nurturing coffee plants, processing beans, and finally brewing. By savoring every sip, we honor the water and effort poured into our beloved beverage."
